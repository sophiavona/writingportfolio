SURF Fellowship · International Health Humanities Consortium
A rhetorical and thematic analysis of 363 AMR news articles from UK outlets, examining how the selective construction of vulnerability stigmatizes non-Western populations and minority patients, and calling for new narratives that foster broader, equitable engagement with antimicrobial resistance.

"Health Securitization and the 2024 Elections: The Past, Present and Future of Antimicrobial Resistance Policy"
PLCY 565: Global Health Law and Policy
Final paper combining literary and policy analysis on AMR in global governance. Analyzes how a One Health framework recognizing the interconnection of human, animal, and environmental health has shaped AMR policy debates in the context of the 2024 elections, and how securitization rhetoric influences political will for this underrecognized global threat. Selected for Presentation at GillingsX, April 2, 2025.
